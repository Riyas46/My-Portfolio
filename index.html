Glossary

Key terms used in this portfolio — for readers outside Atlassian

TermWhat It Means
Rovo AgentAn AI-powered assistant built on Atlassian's Rovo platform. Acts like a knowledgeable colleague that answers questions, retrieves information, and guides users — without making automated decisions. Think of it as a specialist AI chatbot trained on internal Atlassian knowledge.
Rovo Dev CLIAtlassian's AI coding assistant for developers. Used here by a non-engineer (TA practitioner) to independently build HTML tools and Forge apps — without writing a single line of code manually.
Forge AppAn application built natively on Atlassian's Forge platform — Atlassian's secure, cloud-native app development environment. Forge apps live within Atlassian's own infrastructure.
iCIMSAtlassian's Applicant Tracking System (ATS) — the core platform where all candidate records, interview notes, and hiring decisions are documented. Also has a CRM (Candidate Relationship Management) module used for talent events and community building.
EGP (Engineering Growth Profile)Atlassian's internal framework for evaluating and growing engineering talent. Covers four pillars: Project Impact, Engineering Excellence, Direction, and Organisational Impact. The P60 Screener is mapped directly to EGP criteria.
Principal Engineer (P60)A senior individual contributor engineering role at Atlassian — typically 8–12+ years of experience. Equivalent to a Staff or Principal Engineer at most tech companies. Responsible for driving significant technical decisions and cross-team impact.
Senior Software Engineer (P50)A mid-to-senior individual contributor engineering role at Atlassian — typically 5–8 years experience. Equivalent to a Senior Software Engineer at most tech companies.
Senior Engineering Manager (M60)A senior people manager role at Atlassian — managing teams of engineers and often multiple team leads. Equivalent to a Senior Engineering Manager or Director of Engineering at most tech companies.
Staff Engineer (P70)A very senior individual contributor role above Principal Engineer — typically has org-wide or company-wide technical influence. Equivalent to a Distinguished or Fellow Engineer at some companies.
HC (Hiring Committee)Atlassian's structured panel of senior leaders and engineers who make the final hiring decision on candidates. Different from a standard interview — the HC reviews all interview feedback and makes a collective verdict.
ATN (Atlassian Tech Nexus)Atlassian's recurring technology networking events in India — bringing together senior tech professionals from top companies to engage with Atlassian's engineering community. Used as a passive candidate sourcing channel.
PTR (Plan To Revenue / Pipeline To Requirement)A key TA metric measuring whether the sourcing pipeline is on track to meet hiring targets for the quarter or half-year. A healthy PTR indicates enough candidates are in the funnel to hit hiring goals.
HIRE DashboardAtlassian's internal Tableau-based analytics dashboard for TA leaders — showing pipeline health, hiring velocity, offer acceptance rates, and sourcing metrics in real time.
APEXAtlassian's performance review framework — used for evaluating employee contributions across four pillars: Project Impact, Craft Excellence, Direction, and Organisational Impact. Similar to an annual performance review cycle.
Fast PathA streamlined AI governance review track at Atlassian for tools that are knowledge-retrieval only — no automated decisions, no PII processing. HALO and PULSE both applied under Fast Path.
AI Governance ReviewAtlassian's formal review process for AI tools built within the Talent Acquisition team — ensuring every tool is evaluated for compliance, accuracy, and appropriate use before being shared with the broader TA community.
vGHCI / Grace Hopper Celebration IndiaOne of the world's largest gatherings for women in technology, hosted virtually (vGHCI) in FY21. Atlassian participated as an employer partner, represented by Riyas with a keynote presentation.
URG (Under-Represented Group)Atlassian's DEI sourcing category referring to candidates from groups historically underrepresented in the tech industry — including women, people of colour, and other marginalised communities.
